Unlike simple interest, compound interest allows you to earn interest on the complete amount you invest, which is the sum of your capital and interest. This results in an exponential increase in your wealth. The applications of compound interest are virtually limitless in mathematics. It is the lifeblood of most modern businesses. To be sure they are getting the correct return on investment, one needs a trustworthy compound interest calculator.

How can a Compound Interest Calculator Help You?

A worldwide standard technique for calculating the total amount of compound interest accumulated. The equation is

A = P (1 + r/n) ^nt

The following are the variables in the formula:

P Principal Amount
A Compound interest
R/r Rate of interest
N/n Number of times interest compounds in a year
T/t Number of years

For instance, the first year's returns on an investment of Rs. 50,000 with a five-year 10% annual interest rate would be Rs. 5,000 (50,000 x 10/100).

The interest rate for the second year will be based on Rs. 50,000 plus Rs. 5000, or Rs. 55,000. There will be a 5550 rupee interest.

The sum will be Rs 6055 during the third year and so forth.

It goes without saying that manually calculating these numbers is challenging. For this reason, a compound interest calculator in India is necessary to facilitate the process.
